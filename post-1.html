How to write a PhD synopsis (with template)

A clear synopsis helps supervisors and reviewers quickly evaluate your proposal. Here’s a step-by-step approach and a template you can adapt.

What is a synopsis?

A synopsis (or research proposal) is a concise summary of your planned research: the problem, literature, objectives, methodology, and expected contribution. Indian universities typically require 2–8 pages; IGNOU and some institutions may have specific formats — check your university handbook.

Essential sections (and what to write)

  1. Title: Clear, specific and reflects the study domain.
  2. Introduction & background: Brief context and why the problem matters.
  3. Research gap & problem statement: What’s missing in the literature and your focus.
  4. Objectives & research questions: Main objective and 2–4 focused questions.
  5. Methodology: Design (qualitative/quantitative/mixed), sampling, instruments, analysis plan (SPSS/R/Python methods).
  6. Expected outcomes & contribution: What new knowledge will your study add.
  7. Timeline & feasibility: High-level plan and resources needed.
  8. References: Key citations in consistent style.

Tips for Bhopal / Indian universities

  • Follow university formatting and word limits strictly.
  • Emphasize feasibility given local resources and participants.
  • Discuss ethical approvals if human subjects are involved.
